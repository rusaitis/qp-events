Reviewer #1 (Formal Review for Authors (shown to authors)):

This manuscript provides an analysis of observed Quasi-Periodic (QP) Alfvén waves in Saturn's magnetosphere, showing a clear modulation associated with Saturn's Planetary Period Oscillations (PPOs). The methodology of this work seems reliable and provides convincing results, however I have some issues with the interpretation of the results. Additionally I have many minor comments, mostly regarding the presentation of the article.

Major Comments

1. Considering that PPO modulation of the QP waves is considered a key point of this article, it concerns me that there is no discussion of the PPOs within the introduction. They should be discussed within the literature review, with some explanation of how they are (theoretically) generated, and how they effect the current sheet. There is limited explanation for this later in the text, though it absolutely should be included in the introduction.-

We have added discussion on PPO to the introduction. Lines 120-125

MK: The change in the paper is not responsive.  The new material tells us that PPOs exist and who found them but we are asked to discuss how they are generated and how they affect the current sheet. The new material fails to discuss those issues.  Furthermore the Carbary 2016 reference is of the short period periodicities and should be mentioned where those are discussed, not in the discussion of the PPOs. Please go back to the insert and give a proper discussion.

2. One of the key arguments made in this article is that the PPO modulation of the QP waves is due solely to the "flapping" and "breathing" of the equatorial current sheet. However, no argument is made by the authors for how this is physically driving the field line resonance harmonics, and the evidence presented through their analysis does not provide any direct evidence for or against this proposal. In fact, they even refer to an article by Delamere et al. (2015), which combined with their research suggests that magnetodisc reconnection may be the cause of the QP waves.-

“Flapping” and “breathing” is one plausible mechanism for the generation of QP60 waves, but it is not the only possible mechanism. There are other candidates for generating the QP60 waves. We list several in the discussion. In addition, we point out that reconnection is a possible mechanism and note that the waves are most common where the [^c16]reconnection occurs. Lines 649  665

Review: Long-period Alfvén waves as signatures of field line resonances in Saturn's magnetosphere.

Synopsis: This is a welcome addition to a growing series of studies concerned with particle and field signatures of ultra-low frequency plasma waves in Saturn's magnetosphere. It expands on previous studies through a more thorough survey of the Cassini magnetometer data, extending over a wider range in both mission epoch and wave frequency. Importantly, the amplitude of individual Alfven 'wave packets' detected in the data is observed to be further modulated at the 'near-PPO' period associated with systems of rotating current at Saturn. The methodology seems sound and well-described, as do the results and conclusions. Thus my recommendations for the authors are minor in nature.
